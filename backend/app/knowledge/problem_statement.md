## Problem Statement (Jal-Satark)

- Urban flooding in Indian cities has shifted from rare events to chronic, man-made disasters recurring every monsoon.
- Existing flood warning systems (e.g., city-wide systems) often fail to identify street-level **micro-hotspots** where flooding actually accumulates.
- Municipal drainage data is often static and does not reflect **real-time blockages** caused by silt/solid waste during heavy rainfall.
- Current planning is reactive and relies on manual experience rather than a **data-backed readiness index**.
- High-resolution terrain behavior is often ignored, leading to failure in predicting **pooling in micro-depressions** (including very small areas).
- Infrastructure design often assumes ~25 mm/hr rainfall, while modern cloudbursts can exceed **100+ mm/hr**.
